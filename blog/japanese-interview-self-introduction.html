How to structure a Japanese interview self-introduction

A practical framework for a concise, credible introduction in a Japanese job interview.

What the interviewer needs

A useful self-introduction helps the interviewer understand your professional identity, relevant experience, and the connection to the role. It is not your complete life story.

A reliable four-part structure

  1. Your current role or field.
  2. One or two relevant areas of experience.
  3. A concrete strength supported by an example.
  4. A brief connection to the opportunity.

Common mistakes

Avoid listing adjectives without evidence, repeating your résumé word for word, or speaking so long that the interviewer cannot ask follow-up questions.

Practice the next question

After every statement, ask yourself: “What evidence might the interviewer request?” Prepare that example, including the situation, your action, and the result.

Need personal practice?

Use this framework as a starting point, then practice follow-up questions in a realistic conversation.
